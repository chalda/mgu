Instructions:

  1. Open your webbrowser and goto www.smrealms.de
  2. Log into the game.
  3. Enter the current game running.
  4. Open your Alliance page.
  5. Open your Alliance roster page.
  6. On your keyboard, click Ctrl+A, and then Ctrl+C to copy your alliance roster.
  7. On the "Traders Name" tab in this program, hit Ctrl+V to paste your alliance roster.
  8. Return to your Alliance roster page, and click the Alliance Forces link.
  9. On the Alliance Forces page, do the same thing as the Roster (Copy & Paste the Forces page to the Forces tab in this program.
  10. On the "Sectors" tab in this program, it uses the MGU galaxy interface. Navigate in MGU interface to your Alliance's claimed galaxy. This program can handle multiple nrofgals, and if you would like to check around the warp outside of your galaxy, simply switch to that galaxy, and click the desired sectors. The program will remember what sectors you have selected in each galaxy.
  11. If you want to check all the sectors in the galaxy, simply click the Select All button. Otherwise, click each sector you want to check and it will turn from Green to Red. To deselect a sector, simply click the sector again, and it will go back to Green.
  12. Open the "Seeds Report" tab. This tab will give you a list of sectors checked, and a report of who is missing seeds. The Seeds Report is specifically designed to let you know what Alliance mates are missing seeds. There two types of reports: Normal & OP-A
    • OP-A is a report specifically designed for an IRC bot created by RCK. For more info on this, please contact RCK.
    If you would like to copy this report to your Alliance Message Board, simply hit Ctrl+C on your keyboard. (You do not need to select the text from the report, the program knows what tab you are on, and automatically copies the desired report.
  13. Open the "Stacks Report" tab. This report is specifically designed for Miners. It will let the miner know what sizes of stacks are in a sector. This will allow a miner to know what sectors need to be filled. If you would like to copy this report to your Alliance Message Board, simply hit Ctrl+C on your keyboard. (You do not need to select the text from the report, the program knows what tab you are on, and automatically copies the desired report.
  14. Open the "Sectors Report" tab. The Sectors Report was specifically designed to give an idea of your overall strength of your minefield. There two types of reports: Normal & OP-A
    • OP-A is a report specifically designed for an IRC bot created by RCK. For more info on this, please contact RCK.
    If you would like to copy this report to your Alliance Message Board, simply hit Ctrl+C on your keyboard. (You do not need to select the text from the report, the program knows what tab you are on, and automatically copies the desired report.
  15. Open the "Plot Refresh" tab. This tab will attempt to find the most optimized route to refresh/seed with, based on a start sector. You can personalize the route based on where you are at, or you can input your galaxy warp for a more generic route to share with your alliance. There is a "# of variant paths to check" list.
    While trying to find the best route, the program will check the path from sector A, to sector B, C, D, X, Y, and Z. In this scenario, both sector X & Z are adjacent to sector A. So the program will then check the distance of X to B, C, Y, and Z; and also the distance of Z to B, C, D, X and Y. This is to try and find from the two choices (X & Z), what would result in the shortest route after that. This scenario will play out countless times until all possible combinations have been tested. However, when checking say a 6x6 planet galaxy, compared to a 8x8 planet galaxy, the all possible combinations grow exponentially. Due to this, checking all possible combinations could take a very long time, and tie up your CPU.
    To fix this problem, the program lets you specify how many paths to check. Checking 1,000 paths is relatively quick on most PCs, but in most cases will not yield the absolute best route to use. The program allows you to specify how many combinations you wish to check if you desire to get as close to the best route as possible within a reason amount of processing time.
    Having said that, run a plot of more than 25,000 variants at your own risk.
